One complete professional reference

Built for students, technicians, engineers, educators, and ministry production teams.

This handbook connects scientific principles with practical, real-world application. It begins with sound, frequency, hearing, decibels, signal flow, and phase, then advances through microphones, consoles, equalization, dynamics, loudspeakers, live production, studio workflows, networking, system design, troubleshooting, and professional leadership.

Written by Dr. Timothy A. Whitley, the book serves as both a structured classroom textbook and a permanent reference for professionals working in churches, studios, concert venues, broadcast facilities, schools, corporations, and integrated AV environments.

Inside the handbook

A complete learning path from fundamentals to professional practice.

01

Engineering Foundations

Physics of sound, hearing, frequency, decibels, waveforms, phase, and signal flow.

02

Frequency Language

Musical notes, instruments, vocals, harmonics, EQ recognition, and analyzer-based diagnosis.

03

Microphones & Consoles

Polar patterns, placement, preamps, routing, buses, matrices, gain structure, and workflows.

04

EQ & Dynamics

Professional equalization, compression, limiting, gating, de-essing, and tonal control.

05

Live, Studio & Broadcast

Real-world production methods for worship, concerts, recording, streaming, and broadcast.

06

Networks & System Design

Digital audio networks, integration, loudspeaker systems, documentation, safety, and troubleshooting.
